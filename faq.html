Our pricing

We’ve spent a lot of time thinking about this one, and it comes down to a three key factors:

Predictability: Annual per-user pricing enables our customers to budget clearly without needing to itemize per-project software costs.

Positive Incentives: Unlimited usage ensures engineers use the platform fully, without resorting to inefficient workarounds.

Price: We incur costs on a user-by-user basis through services like support and onboarding. Conversely, the data storage costs we incur on a log-by-log basis are functionally zero. Why should we bill you for that?
This one comes down to incentives. If we charged you a flat, one-time fee, our company would be incentivized to sell hard and disregard current users. Conversely, a subscription model ensures that we continue to provide excellent service and roll out fantastic features to keep our customers onboard.
We’re more than a software company — we’re a data management company. Our UI saves time (often 75% faster than gINT), but the real value is in how we structure and manage data over years.

Concurrent licensing works for tools where value is tied to time in-app, like CAD. In our case, an engineer might spend just five minutes viewing a map — yet that insight relies on years of data work. And we never want to block engineers from working because all seats are taken, especially during peak times.
We do, but paying annually comes with a 17% discount!
We’re software developers, which means we’re used to quickly testing new things, iterating, and improving.

Our goal is for BoreDM to help as many engineers as possible. If we could change our pricing model to make BoreDM a better fit for your business, let us know and we’ll explore another approach with your team. We learn from our users every day.
